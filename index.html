A magnitude 7.8 earthquake struck the southern coast of Mindanao at 11:37 p.m. UTC on June 7, 2026, causing widespread damage to communities, infrastructure, and property across four regions of the southern Philippines.

According to the United States Geological Survey (USGS), the epicenter was located approximately 16 miles west-southwest of Kablalan at a depth of 55 km deep enough to spread shaking across a wide area but shallow enough to cause significant surface damage. The rupture was the result of thrust faulting near the convergence of the Cotabato Trench and the Sangihe Trench.

Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) recorded a maximum Modified Mercalli Intensity of VIII (Severe), meaning most structures suffered significant damage and ground movement was violent enough to topple objects and crack masonry. The primary shock lasted approximately 30 seconds and triggered more than 180 aftershocks, including a M6.5 secondary event.
